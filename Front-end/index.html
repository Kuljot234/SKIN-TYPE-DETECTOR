What are Skin Diseases?

Skin diseases are conditions that affect your skin. These diseases may cause rashes, inflammation, itchiness or other skin changes. Some skin conditions may be genetic, while lifestyle factors may cause others. Skin disease treatment may include medications, creams or ointments, or lifestyle changes.

Skin Type and Skin Diseases

Oily Skin

Oily skin occurs due to excess sebum production, leading to shine, clogged pores, and acne. Manage it with gentle cleansers, lightweight moisturizers, clay masks, blotting papers, and a balanced diet.

Dry Skin

Dry skin occurs when the skin lacks moisture or natural oils, leading to flakiness, itching, and tightness. Manage it with gentle cleansers, rich moisturizers, hydrating serums, and avoiding harsh products.

Normal Skin

Balanced skin with minimal dryness or oiliness, smooth texture, and few imperfections. Maintain it with a gentle cleanser, lightweight moisturizer, sunscreen, and occasional exfoliation for healthy skin.

Combination skin

Features both oily (often T-zone) and dry areas. Use gentle cleansers, balance hydration with lightweight moisturizers, and treat specific areas (e.g., mattifying products for oily spots, richer creams for dryness).
